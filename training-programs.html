Training programs

Fitness training balances five elements of good health. Make sure your routine includes aerobic fitness, strength training, core exercises, and flexibility and stretching.

Cardio Program

Cardio

Cardio exercise is, as the name suggests, a way to benefit your cardiovascular system. Studies have proven that increased levels of exercise can benefit your heart and lower your risk of cardiovascular diseases. A 2021 study even found that the benefits of physical activity on cardiovascular health are limitless. Study authors found that there is no threshold at which physical activity does not benefit the heart.

Details

Aerobic activity, also known as cardio or endurance activity, is the cornerstone of most fitness training programs. Aerobic activity or exercise causes you to breathe faster and more deeply, which maximizes the amount of oxygen in your blood.

Strength Training

Strength and Fitness

Muscular fitness is another key component of a fitness training program. Strength training can help you increase bone strength and muscular fitness, and it can help you manage or lose weight. It can also improve your ability to do everyday activities.

Details

Aim is to include strength training of all the major muscle groups into your fitness routine at least twice a week. Hand-held weights or homemade weights — such as plastic soft drink bottles filled with water or sand — may work just as well. Resistance bands are another inexpensive option. Your own body weight counts, too. Try pushups, pullups, abdominal crunches and leg squats.

Functionality program

Functional Traning

The beauty of functional training or functional fitness lies in the fact that these exercises equip the doer to perform daily tasks better. By increasing muscle stability, joint mobilisation, and boosting full-body strength, functional exercises also help prevent injury when you’re out and about your everyday tasks, be it moving boxes, picking up heavy objects, or simply keeping your balance on the uneven, unstable ground.

Details

Functional fitness exercises, as the name suggests, are functional in nature. They are designed based on the complex, multi-chain movements the body uses every day in various situations. Fitness professional and nutritionist Neha Godiawala Shah says, “The importance of functional training is that it trains the entire body for everyday activities. These exercises are good for individuals of all ages and fitness levels, while also being popular among bodybuilders and other athletes.”
